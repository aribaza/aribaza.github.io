Overview

PillNet addresses the challenge of identifying pills in a world moving away from single-use packaging. By scanning a pill, the app provides its type and details within seconds, aiding users in medication recognition and management.

Key Features

  • Simple graphical user interface with large text and minimal navigation.
  • Text-to-speech functionality with adjustable volume, ideal for visually impaired users.
  • Accurate pill identification supported by a trained neural network.
  • Expandable database for continuous improvement and broad coverage.

Neural Network Training

Our training process utilized the GoogleNet architecture, with a dataset of 150 pill images split into 80% for training and 20% for testing. Post-training, the model achieved a 100% success rate on test data and performed robustly during integration tests with webcam input.

Project Evaluation

Through feasibility, desirability, and viability analysis, we determined:

  • Feasibility: Expanding the app to include a larger pill database is practical and achievable.
  • Desirability: While not universally desired, the app proves invaluable for the target user persona.
  • Viability: Further training using diverse backgrounds and lighting conditions will enhance real-world performance.

Future Potential

PillNet demonstrates strong promise for improving medication safety and accessibility. With further exploration and development, it can significantly enhance healthcare management for diverse user groups.
