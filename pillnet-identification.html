PillNet: Accessible Neural Network-Based Pill Identification App

Empowering users to identify pills using a neural network-based mobile app for accurate, fast, and accessible identification.

Overview

PillNet is an innovative mobile app that leverages a neural network to identify pills based on image input. The app allows users to take a picture of a pill, and within seconds, it provides a possible identification, including the pill's name, dosage, and manufacturer. This tool is especially beneficial for people with limited access to healthcare professionals and can help reduce medication errors.

The Approach

The app uses deep learning techniques to train a neural network model that recognizes pills from a large database of images. The model is optimized to handle different shapes, sizes, and colors of pills. The app integrates a simple, user-friendly interface that makes it easy for anyone to take a photo and receive instant identification results.

Key Features

  • Accurate pill identification based on a photo, using a trained neural network model.
  • Real-time feedback on pill identification results, including the name, dosage, and manufacturer.
  • Integration with a large, continuously updated pill database.
  • Accessibility features, including voice feedback and large text for those with visual impairments.

Technologies Used

  • Deep learning for image recognition, using convolutional neural networks (CNNs).
  • Mobile app developed with React Native for cross-platform compatibility (iOS and Android).
  • Backend powered by Firebase to store pill data and user interactions.
  • Cloud storage for storing high-quality images of pills used for training the neural network.

Conclusion

PillNet aims to make pill identification quick and accessible to everyone, helping users identify their medication accurately and confidently. By integrating AI into healthcare applications, PillNet plays a critical role in empowering individuals to take control of their health while ensuring that they avoid potential medication errors.
